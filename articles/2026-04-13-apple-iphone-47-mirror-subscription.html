Apple Unveils iPhone 47: It's Just a Mirror With a $2,499 Monthly Subscription

The tech giant's latest innovation removes every feature except the one Apple says consumers truly want: the ability to look at themselves. Wall Street is thrilled. Your wallet is not.

By DIANA CLICKSWORTH April 13, 2026 | 4 min read
SHARE:

CUPERTINO, CA - Apple CEO Tim Cook took the stage at Apple Park on Tuesday to unveil the company's most ambitious product yet: the iPhone 47, a device that the company describes as "a revolutionary new way to experience yourself." The device, which is functionally a mirror with rounded corners, retails for $3,999 with a mandatory $2,499 monthly subscription for what Apple is calling "Reflection+".

"This is the most personal device we've ever created," Cook told a crowd of visibly confused journalists and inexplicably enthusiastic Apple employees. "Because it's literally you. You are the content. You are the experience. You are the app. And starting today, you can subscribe to yourself for just $2,499 a month."

The crowd applauded. A woman in the third row began to weep. It was unclear whether this was from joy or from reading the spec sheet.

Features That Aren't Features

According to Apple's press materials, the iPhone 47 boasts the following specifications:

  • Display: 6.7-inch Reflective Retina Surface (it's glass)
  • Camera: None. "You already know what you look like," said Apple VP of Product Marketing Karen Luu.
  • Storage: 0 GB. "Your memories are stored in your brain. We're just helping you access them organically."
  • Battery: None required. "The iPhone 47 is always on, as long as there is light in the room."
  • Processor: Your own consciousness. Apple calls this the "A0 Biochip."
  • Weight: "The weight of your own self-image" (approximately 184 grams)

The device does ship with one pre-installed app: Self-Awareness, which Apple describes as "a mindfulness tool that encourages users to confront their own reflection without the distraction of notifications, social media, or functioning technology." The app costs an additional $9.99 per month.

"We asked ourselves: what if the phone didn't do anything? And the answer was a $4 billion quarter."

Wall Street Loves It

Despite the product's complete lack of functionality, Apple shares surged 14% in after-hours trading following the announcement. Analysts at Goldman Sachs issued a "Strong Buy" rating, with senior tech analyst Richard Bellweather writing in a note to clients: "Apple has finally achieved the dream of every subscription business: charging money for nothing."

"The margins on this product are extraordinary," Bellweather continued. "The bill of materials is essentially a piece of glass and a brushed aluminum frame. We estimate Apple's gross margin on the iPhone 47 at approximately 97.3%. This is not a phone. This is a masterpiece of capitalism."

Samsung immediately announced plans to release its own version, the Galaxy Mirror S47, which will reportedly do the same thing but with a foldable screen and a stylus that is, technically, a pencil.

Editor's Note: Samsung's press release included the phrase "our mirror folds." We have reached out for clarification on why this is necessary. Samsung has not responded.

The Accessories Ecosystem

Apple also announced a full line of accessories for the iPhone 47, including:

  • The MirrorCase Pro ($199) - a case that protects your mirror from scratches while also being a slightly smaller mirror
  • AirPods Mirror Edition ($349) - earbuds that play the sound of your own breathing back to you in spatial audio
  • The Apple Cloth 2 ($49) - an updated version of Apple's polishing cloth, now with its own subscription model ($4.99/month for "premium wipe patterns")
  • MagSafe Mirror Stand ($129) - props the mirror up on your desk so you can look at yourself during meetings

Apple Genius Bar employees have been retrained to handle the new product's most common support request, which during beta testing was: "How do I turn this on?" The official answer, per internal documentation obtained by News Tabloid Now, is: "It's already on. That's you. You're looking at you."

Consumer Reaction

Lines have already begun forming outside Apple Stores in New York, San Francisco, and London, with some customers camping out despite not fully understanding what they're waiting for.

"I've had every iPhone since the original," said Marcus Chen, 34, who was first in line at the Fifth Avenue store. "I don't care what it does. I don't care if it does nothing. I've already sold my current phone on eBay to fund this purchase. Also, do you know where the nearest library is? I need to use their WiFi to look something up."

A smaller but vocal group of critics has emerged on social media, with the hashtag #MirrorGate trending briefly before being overtaken by #MirrorGateIsBeautiful, which Apple's marketing team insists emerged organically.

The iPhone 47 ships on May 1st. Pre-orders require a credit check, a brief psychological evaluation, and a signed waiver acknowledging that "the device does not make phone calls, send messages, or connect to the internet, and Apple is not responsible for any existential crises that may result from prolonged use."

[CORRECTION]: This article originally stated the iPhone 47 costs $3,999. This is the base price. The "iPhone 47 Pro Max Ultra" variant, which includes a slightly larger mirror and a leather carrying pouch, costs $5,499. We regret the omission.
